Blockchain

Blockchain technology is the most emerging markets of todays. It is an open, distributed ledger that can record transactions between two parties efficiently and in a verifiable and permanent way. For use as a distributed ledger, blockchain is typically managed by a peer to peer network. It's one of the most secure technology and inevitable to changes. This all works on the basis of central ledger system.

Data analytics

Data analytics (DA) is the process of examining data sets in order to draw conclusions about the information they contain, increasingly with the aid of specialized systems and software. Data analytics technologies and techniques are widely used in commercial industries to enable organizations to make more-informed business decisions and by scientists and researchers to verify or disprove scientific models, theories and hypotheses.

Machine learning

Machine learning (ML) is the scientific study of algorithms and statistical models that computer systems use to progressively improve their performance on a specific task. Machine learning algorithms are used in the applications of email filtering, detection of network intruders, and computer vision, where it is infeasible to develop an algorithm of specific instructions for performing the task. It's closely related to computational statistics, which focuses on making predictions using computers. It's applications includes business models, robotics, medical field, smart cities and IoT devices.

Application Development

Android is a mobile operating system developed by Google. It is based on a modified version of the Linux kernel and other open source software, and is designed primarily for touchscreen mobile devices. In addition, Google has further developed Android TV for televisions, Android car for cars, and Wear OS for wrist watches, each with a specialized user interface. Variants of Android are also used on game Consoles, electronics, IoT devices etc.

Augmented reality (AR)

Augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) technology is not entirely new—the first personal headset was developed in 1968. The technology has continued to develop, driven by gaming, entertainment, scientific, education, medical and military applications. More recently, we have seen the introduction of exciting new consumer headsets, geared toward gaming and other virtual experiences, such as 360-degree video.

Artificial intelligence(AI)

Artificial intelligence(AI), sometimes called machine intelligence, is intelligence demonstrated by machine, in contrast to the natural intelligence displayed by humans and other animals. AI system has ability to correctly interpret external data, to learn from such data, and to use those learnings to achieve specific goals and tasks through flexible adaptation. AI has elements from cognitive as well as emotional intelligence, social intelligence and is widely used in decision making and robotics.

Single Secured Distributed Identity System

Given the immense sensitivity of Personal data, this data is encrypted and stored in a private ledger using SSID portal. Users will be provided a unique SSID that gives users complete ownership over his/her identity and personal data. Any company/agency wanting user (personal) data will have to seek fresh consent from the user instead of buying data from other companies. Users will also be able to view the SSID portal if he/she has granted the permission to an agency to use their data.

Datacops Analytics Platform

This Platform provides comprehensive analysis of 15 years of compiled data for all States/Uts and Police Districts for Crime against Women through use of advanced data analytics. It uses machine learning to project trends and predict crime hotspots in a police district. The multivariate model built for prediction of crime gives accuracy of over 91%. An example of useful insights is: Trafficking of women happens primarily in coastal states. Further, appointing women at the SI rank by filling available vacancies leads to about 80% reduction in trafficking of women. Several similar insights can reduce crimes significantly.

Safer Routes

Safer Routes feature identifies crime affected areas in the city at different time of the day and helps citizens avoid such areas & routes them towards safer routes. It also helps police identify areas for frequent patrolling & improving road lighting or surveillance capabilities, This feature can then be integrated with widely used services such as Google Maps, taxi hailing Apps like Ola or Uber, Tourism and Hospitality services and more, where users can use this features to avoid areas of High Crime density at varied Time of the day.
